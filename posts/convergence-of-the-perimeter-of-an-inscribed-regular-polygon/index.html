Convergence of the perimeter of an inscribed regular polygon

March 18, 2025

Consider a nnn-sided regular polygon inscribed in a circle of radius rrr. The summits of the polygon lie on the circle, and all sides of the polygon are of equal length. The angle at the center of a regular polygon is the angle formed by two consecutive rays of the circle passing through the vertices of the polygon. Since the polygon is regular, all these angles at the center are equal.

  • Full circle circumference : The complete circumference of a circle is 2π2\pi2π radians.

  • Number of sides: The polygon has nnn sides.

  • Circumference division: Each side of the polygon subtends an angle at the center that is a fraction of the total circumference. Since the polygon has nnn sides, the circumference is divided into nnn equal parts.

Therefore, the centrale angle subtended by a side of the polygon is :

Central angle=2πn radians\text{Central angle} = \frac{2\pi}{n} \text{ radians}Central angle=n2π​ radians

Example :

For n=6n = 6n=6 (hexagon), each side subtends an angle at the center of : 2π6=π3 radians\frac{2\pi}{6} = \frac{\pi}{3} \text{ radians}62π​=3π​ radians

Thanks to the inscribed angle theorem , we know that :

Base angle=Central angle2=πn\text{Base angle} = \frac{\text{Central angle}}{2} = \frac{\pi}{n}Base angle=2Central angle​=nπ​

Let’s take the isosceles triangle OABOABOAB where :

  • OOO is the center of the circle.

  • AAA and BBB are two consecutive vertices of the polygon.

  • OAOAOA and OBOBOB are the radii of the circle, each of length rrr.

  • ABABAB is a side of the polygon, of length sss.

We have proved that :

  • The centrale angle ∠AOB=2πn\angle AOB = \frac{2\pi}{n}∠AOB=n2π​

  • The hypotenuse is the radius OAOAOA, which is rrr.

We now have all we need to demonstrate the value of the perimeter of a polygon.

The perimeter PPP of the polygon is the sum of the lengths of all its sides. Since the polygon has nnn sides, each of length sss, the perimeter is :

P=n⋅s=n⋅2rsin⁡(πn)P = n \cdot s = n \cdot 2r \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{n}\right)P=n⋅s=n⋅2rsin(nπ​)

The basic question was to find out the value of the perimeter PPP of the inscribed regular polygon when the number of its sides nnn tends towards infinity.

lim⁡n→∞P=2πr\lim\limits_{n \to \infty} P = 2\pi rn→∞lim​P=2πr

sin⁡(πn)≈πn\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{n}\right) \approx \frac{\pi}{n}sin(nπ​)≈nπ​
